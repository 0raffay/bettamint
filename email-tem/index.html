Consumer Behavioral Learnings
1 Smaller contractors - employing a maximum 50- 250 workers enjoy using the application and have also mandated it for use across all their construction sites. It helps them save 1-2 hours of time usually spent in administrative work every day.
2 Our existing enterprise clients are referring other customers and helping us build a strong pipeline at near to negligible CAC.
3 98% of the construction workers use the mobile application in English despite it being available in regional languages.
4 We successfully created ’tech leaders’ on each construction site. They are construction workers usually in the age group of 20-30 who are very tech savvy. They help us onboard all the workers on their site, troubleshoot issues and we reward them based on a sales incentive plan.
5 One of our clients requested for us to go live with wage payment distribution to enable him to pay 2000 workers. This will be tested next quarter.
   

Product Updates
We initially had only 2 interfaces, a desktop app for employers and a mobile app for workers. We will are now live on the play store with the employer app. This was crucial for workforce management on field
Product features added: Muster Roll, Universal Muster Card, Worker ID cards, QR codes to verify workers/jobs , Daily Labour Reports, Daily Attendance Reports, Nominal Muster Roll, Subskills for workers, Billing and Subscription Dashboards.

Where We Fell Short
1 We were busy bug fixing most of Q1 2023 and didn’t have the bandwidth to launch the wage distribution payment module. This is the entire focus for Q2 2023
2 It is time-consuming to onboard users. We are at our maximum capacity of 75 users a day. We are developing a tool that allows us to onboard 1000’s of users in less than 5 minutes. This will greatly simplify the process of onboarding workers at existing worksites
3 The app crashed several times, needed many updates and this frustrated the construction workers to have to keep updating the application or not being able to check in to work. This is now behind us.
